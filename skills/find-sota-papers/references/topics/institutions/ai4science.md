# Topic key: `ai4science`

**Matches** AI for science, ML for physics and chemistry, materials discovery,
ML interatomic potentials, weather and climate foundation models, PDE / neural
surrogates, and scientific computing.

**Applied in addition to `general.md`, never instead of it.** Read this file only
when the user's `topics` match the key above; an AI-for-science run's accept-list
is the 147 general entries **plus** the 9 below.

These are national laboratories and forecasting/physics centers whose AI-for-science
output is strong enough to matter for such a search but that do not rank into the
general list on overall AI volume — the compute-and-simulation institutions a
CSRankings-shaped list never mentions. None duplicates a general-list entry (the
broad-excellence band already carries Caltech, Max Planck Society, Helmholtz,
CNRS, and Paris-Saclay, so those are not repeated here).

| Institution | Country/Region | AI-for-science focus |
|---|---|---|
| ECMWF | Europe (UK / Germany / Italy) | AI-based medium-range weather forecasting (AIFS) and reanalysis |
| Argonne National Laboratory | USA | Exascale HPC and AI surrogates for science |
| Oak Ridge National Laboratory | USA | Leadership computing (Frontier); AI for materials and energy |
| Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory | USA | Materials Project, ML interatomic potentials, scientific computing (NERSC) |
| Pacific Northwest National Laboratory | USA | Computational chemistry and materials |
| Los Alamos National Laboratory | USA | Computational physics and materials |
| National Center for Atmospheric Research (NCAR) | USA | Climate and Earth-system modeling |
| Flatiron Institute — Center for Computational Quantum Physics | USA | ML for quantum many-body systems and astrophysics |
| CERN | Switzerland | Machine learning for particle-physics data |
