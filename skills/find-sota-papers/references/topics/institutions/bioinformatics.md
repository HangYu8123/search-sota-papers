# Topic key: `bioinformatics`

**Matches** computational biology, bioinformatics, protein and genomics
foundation models, protein structure prediction, sequence analysis, single-cell
and multi-omics, and molecular / de-novo design.

**Applied in addition to `general.md`, never instead of it.** Read this file only
when the user's `topics` match the key above; a bioinformatics run's accept-list
is the 147 general entries **plus** the 9 below.

These are organizations whose computational-biology output is strong enough to
matter for a bioinformatics search but that do not rank into the general list on
overall AI volume — dedicated sequence-and-structure institutes and genome
centers that a CSRankings-shaped list never mentions. None of them duplicates a
general-list entry (the general list already carries the broad biomedical band —
Harvard, UCSF, NIH, Karolinska, Rockefeller, Weizmann — so those are not repeated
here).

| Institution | Country/Region | Bioinformatics focus |
|---|---|---|
| European Bioinformatics Institute (EMBL-EBI) | UK | Open biological data resources; sequence, structure, and functional-genomics analysis |
| Wellcome Sanger Institute | UK | Large-scale genome sequencing; cancer and cellular genomics |
| Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard | USA | Genomics methods, single-cell atlases, and computational tools (GATK) |
| Flatiron Institute — Center for Computational Biology | USA | Computational genomics, single-cell, and biophysics |
| European Molecular Biology Laboratory (EMBL) | Germany | Molecular biology, bioimage analysis, and computational modeling |
| Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory | USA | Quantitative genomics and sequence analysis |
| The Jackson Laboratory | USA | Computational genetics and mammalian genomics |
| BGI Research | China | High-throughput sequencing and population-scale genomics |
| Institut Pasteur | France | Bioinformatics of infectious disease and microbial genomics |

> EMBL-EBI is EMBL's bioinformatics outstation in the UK; an author block naming
> either counts. See the matching rules in `../institutions.md`.
