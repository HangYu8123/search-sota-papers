# Topic key: `ai-safety`

**Matches** AI safety, alignment, mechanistic interpretability, sparse
autoencoders / feature analysis, activation steering, scalable oversight,
dangerous-capability evaluations, and AI control.

**Applied in addition to `general.md`, never instead of it.** Read this file only
when the user's `topics` match the key above; an AI-safety run's accept-list is
the 147 general entries **plus** the 10 below.

These are safety-and-interpretability labs, evaluation organizations, and
nonprofits whose alignment output is strong enough to matter for such a search but
that do not rank into the general list on overall AI volume. None duplicates a
general-list entry.

| Institution | Country/Region | AI-safety focus |
|---|---|---|
| Anthropic | USA | Mechanistic interpretability, scalable oversight, alignment |
| Redwood Research | USA | AI control and alignment under adversarial assumptions |
| Apollo Research | UK | Interpretability and evaluations for deceptive behavior |
| EleutherAI | USA | Open-source interpretability and model analysis |
| METR | USA | Dangerous-capability and autonomy evaluations |
| UK AI Safety Institute (AISI) | UK | Independent frontier-model evaluations |
| Center for AI Safety (CAIS) | USA | Safety benchmarks and risk research |
| Alignment Research Center (ARC) | USA | Theoretical alignment and capability elicitation |
| FAR AI | USA | Alignment research and red-teaming |
| Goodfire | USA | Interpretability tooling and model editing |

> Several of these (notably Anthropic) also publish frontier-capability work; they
> are listed here for their safety and interpretability output. Frontier labs
> already accepted through `general.md` count on this topic without appearing here.
